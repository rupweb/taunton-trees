Juniper Tree

A more detailed description of these evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs. Coming from the Artic Circle to Mount Kenya on the Equator, they are probably the most widely distributed of all trees and shrubs in nature


Juniperus communis 'Hibernica' - Irish Juniper

Description

A dense growing and compact but slender, columnar form with densely arranged leaves. A very popular conifer, excellent for formal gardens. The fruits are rounded, fleshy and berry-like. They are very versatile and include plants for most soils and situations. They range in colour from green to yellow, grey and steel blue.

Suggested Uses

Formal garden shrub and hedging tree. Many creative possibilities for patterned arrangements. Can be clipped and/or shaped.

Size

From 8 feet high
